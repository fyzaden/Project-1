Mustafa Kemal ATATURK

Leader of National Struggle and Modernization

Here's a time line of ATATURK's life:

  • 1881 - Mustafa Kemal Atatürk was born in Thessaloniki, Greece
  • 1888 – 1893 - Mustafa, who started his education life, first went to Hafız Mehmed Efendi Mahalle School and then to Şemsi Efendi School and finished primary school there.
  • 1893 - Mustafa Kemal took the Thessaloniki Military Junior High School exams, secretly from his mother, and after passing these exams, he started to receive secondary school education at this school.
  • 1896 - He entered Monastir Military Preparatory School for high school education.
  • 13 March 1899 - Mustafa Kemal, who left Thessaloniki and came to Istanbul, entered the Istanbul Military Academy and graduated from there with the rank of lieutenant.
  • February 10, 1902 - Mustafa Kemal, who studied at the Military Academy after the Military School, graduated from there as a staff captain in 1905.
  • January 11, 1905 - He started his duty in the 5th Army, headquartered in Damascus.
  • 1906 - Mustafa Kemal, together with his friends, founded the "Homeland and Freedom Society" in Damascus
  • 1909 - He was appointed Chief of Staff of the Salonika Redif Division, which was affiliated with the 3rd Army. In the same year, the March 31 Incident took place in Istanbul. The Movement Army under the command of Mahmut Şevket Pasha started to march from Thessaloniki to Istanbul. Mustafa Kemal was the chief of staff of this army
  • September 13, 1911 -He was assigned to the General Staff Headquarters in Istanbul.
  • October 5, 1911 - He participated in the Tripoli War and fought against the Italian invaders, especially in Tobruk and Derna.
  • 27 November 1911 - Mustafa Kemal became a major while he was in Tripoli.
  • October 8, 1912 - Following the outbreak of the Balkan War, he was appointed as the director of the operations branch of the corps established in Bolayır.
  • 1913 - He helped take Edirne back from the Bulgarians in the 2nd Balkan War.
  • 1914 - Promoted to lieutenant colonel.
  • 1915 - He was very effective in winning the Gallipoli Wars.
  • 1916 - Promoted to Brigadier General.
  • 16 March 1616 - He went to Diyarbakır with the 16th Corps, of which he was appointed commander. Fought on the Caucasus Front against the Russians
  • 1917 - He was appointed as the Commander of the 7th Army under the command of the Yıldırım Army Group. He fought against the British on the Palestine Front of World War I.
  • 1918 - Mudros returned to Istanbul after the Armistice Agreement was signed.
  • 1919 - He was appointed as 9th Army Inspector and landed in Samsun on 19 May 1919.
  • May 28, 1919 - Havza started the War of Independence by publishing the circular.
  • 1919 - Amasya Circular, Erzurum Congress and Sivas Congress determined the purpose and management of the Turkish War of Independence.
  • 27 December 1919 - He came to Ankara
  • April 23, 1920 - Turkish Grand National Assembly was opened.
  • 1921 - The 1st Inönü, 2nd İnönü and Sakarya Battles were won against the Greek occupation forces.
  • September 9, 1921 - The Turkish army entered Izmir and Izmir was liberated from the Greek occupation.
  • 1922 - The Great Offensive, which started on August 26, ended with the decisive defeat of the Greeks on August 30.
  • 11 October 1922 - Mudanya Armistice Agreement was signed with the Allied Powers.
  • July 24, 1923 - Lausanne Peace Treaty was signed.
  • 29 October 1923- The Republic was declared.
  • 1923 – 1938 -After the proclamation of the Republic and the declaration of the new Republic of Turkey, a great restoration period began. A series of reforms were carried out in multiple fields, including education, law, economic and social life, and great efforts were made to raise this new republic to the level of contemporary civilizations. With these revolutions, the Republic of Turkey; It has become one of the most respected states in the world.
  • 1938 - Mustafa Kemal Atatürk died on the morning of November 10, 1938.
